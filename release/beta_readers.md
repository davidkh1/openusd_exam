# Beta Reader Guide

Thanks for reading the **OpenUSD Exam Companion** beta. The book is
content-complete and every code example is machine-verified — what we need
from humans is everything machines can't judge.

## What to send feedback on (in priority order)

1. **Technical wrongness.** Anything you believe is incorrect about USD
   behavior — especially if you can show a snippet that disproves it.
2. **Unclear explanations.** Anywhere you had to re-read, or where a term
   appears before it's explained.
3. **The mock exam experience.** Take it under exam conditions (90 minutes,
   closed book). Send: your total, your per-domain scores from the key's
   table, how the time pressure felt, and any question that seemed
   ambiguous or had more than one defensible answer.
4. **Drills.** Were the inline solutions placed well (visible too early?
   helpful?), and did any drill feel busy-work rather than learning?
5. **Density check.** Any section that felt like filler — name it, we cut it.
6. **For certified readers:** does the emphasis match the real exam's, by
   domain?

## What not to spend time on

- Typos and layout glitches — send them, but don't hunt them; the book is
  still being revised.
- The cover — placeholder.

## How to send

- GitHub issues: https://github.com/davidkh1/openusd_exam/issues
  (one issue per topic, please — page number + quote)
- Or email the author directly.
